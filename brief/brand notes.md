# Brand basics

## Colors

Main bg color: #211E1E
Main text color: #fff
Primary color (links, buttons, decorations etc.): #BF192B

### Flat colors — no gradients
- The whole design is **flat**: solid fills only. **No gradients, no glows, no soft drop-shadows.**
- If more than the three core colors are needed (e.g. for hover states, borders, muted/"Information kommer" text, tinted event backgrounds), build them as **tints/shades of the existing colors** — a small scale derived from `#211E1E` / `#fff` / `#BF192B` — not new hues.
- Goal: it should look **deliberately designed, not "AI-generated"**. Avoid the generic look — purple/blue gradients, glassmorphism, glossy buttons, drop-shadowed cards floating on gradients. Keep it bold, flat and brand-true (the marketing pattern is the reference).


## Typography 

Headings: MBJ Chynky (White with a #211E1E border if placed upon other colors than bg color. Thickness about 20-25% of font size.)
Main text, preamble etc: IBM Plex Mono

## Visual language — decoration pattern

The recurring brand signature (see `marketing examples/`: `marketing.jpg`, `schedule-mobile-bg.jpg`, `stall-tag-example.jpg`) is a dense, hand-drawn pattern of red motifs on the dark background, with the club logo and the main message sitting on top.

- **Motifs**: moons, stars (filled + outline), pine trees, snowflakes, plus signs, wavy lines, dots, arrows/asterisks, plus rotated/curved "text chips" of club sayings and references — e.g. "IKVÄLL KÄKAR VI PUCK!", "DÄR STJÄRNOR TÄNDS", "77-KEDJAN", "REDMILL SUPPORT", "PITEKVARTEN", "1986", local bridge names ("Pisundssbron" …).
- **Composition**: motifs fill the whole surface at varying scale/rotation as a background; the logo is centered/prominent; the headline uses MBJ Chunky in white. The `stall-tag-example` (logo + large chunky name + small mono subline over the pattern) is the direct model for the home-page group cards.
- **Color**: the pattern and all other red usage on the web use `#BF192B` (decided — this is the single red, matching the pattern art; the older `#F8233A` is not used).

## Tone of voice

Reference: `marketing examples/invitation tonality.txt` (source for *tone*, not for the actual site copy).

- Warm, welcoming and proud of tradition ("traditionsenligt", "vår mycket populära hockeyskola").
- Direct, personal address to parents/players ("du", "Er", "ditt barn").
- Informative and clear about practicalities, but never stiff or corporate.
- Swedish throughout, upbeat and encouraging.
